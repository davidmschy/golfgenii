Before Bryson DeChambeau became a household name -- before the U.S. Open, before the Masters, before the long drive records -- there was a coach in California named Mike Schy who saw something nobody else did.

In 2015, Mike guided Bryson to both the NCAA Division I Championship and the U.S. Amateur title in the same calendar year. That had not been done in decades. It remains one of the most celebrated accomplishments in amateur golf history.
